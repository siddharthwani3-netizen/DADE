Why College Degree Alone Isn’t Enough to Land a Job

Every year, only 10–15% of tech graduates land jobs right after college—the rest face rejection.

College doesn't make you job-ready

Students graduate with theory-heavy knowledge but lack the hands-on skills required in real-world jobs.

No real exposure to how tech teams work

Most learners have never worked in a team, followed agile processes, or contributed to a product — making the transition to a job overwhelming.

First jobs feel like a second education

Freshers spend their first few months learning what they should’ve already known — instead of contributing from day one, leading to wasted hours of tech leads and eventually impacting the company’s growth.

Lack of guidance slows growth

Tech managers rarely have time for one-on-one training, leaving most freshers stuck in their careers.

Until you train the way companies actually work, you’ll always feel unprepared.
